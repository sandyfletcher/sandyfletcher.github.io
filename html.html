Breakfast

Deviled Eggs
  • 12 eggs
  • 3 tsp dijon mustard
  • ⅓ cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp minced shallot / onion
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ⅛ tsp salt
  • ⅛ tsp pepper
  • ¼ tsp tabasco sauce
  • salt and pepper
  • paprika + chives + pickle juice
  1. Place eggs in a large saucepan and cover with water.
  2. Transfer to stovetop over high heat until water begins to boil.
  3. Boil for one minute, cover with lid, and remove from heat. Allow to sit for 17 minutes and then drain and transfer to an ice bath.
  4. Peel eggs and set aside.
  5. Slice each egg in half lengthwise and gently remove yolk halves into small mixing bowl. Arrange egg whites on serving platter.
  6. Use a fork to mash the yolks together with mustard, mayonnaise, onion, tabasco, salt, and pepper.
  7. Add mayonnaise as necessary to get desired creamy consistency, then piping bag or spoon mixture back into egg halves.
  8. Sprinkle with paprika and let settle.

Bread

Loaf Pan Bread
  • 2 cups warm water
  • 1 tbsp active dry yeast
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 4-5½ cups bread flour
  • Butter for brushing
  1. Combine water, yeast and a pinch of sugar in a large bowl.
  2. Allow to rest for 5-10 minutes, until foaming.
  3. Add remaining sugar, salt, oil and 3 cups of flour, mixing to combine.
  4. Add another cup of flour and mix to combine, continuing to add more, ½ cup at a time, until the dough begins to pull away from the sides of the bowl.
  5. The dough should be smooth and elastic and slightly stick to a clean finger, but not be overly sticky. Add a little more flour if needed.
  6. Mix the dough for 5 minutes on a lightly floured surface.
  7. Grease a large bowl with oil and place the dough inside, turning to coat.
  8. Cover with a dish towel or plastic wrap and allow to rise in a warm place until doubled in size, about 1 ½ hours.
  9. Oil two 9x5'' bread pans generously and add a small piece of parchment paper at the bottom if the pan sticks.
  10. Punch the dough down well to remove air bubbles, shape into two long logs and place into prepared loaf pans.
  11. Cover loosely with plastic wrap and allow dough to rise again until an inch above the top of the pan, about 45 minutes.
  12. Remove plastic wrap and preheat oven to 350°F.
  13. Bake for about 30 minutes or until golden brown on top - should sound hollow when tapped.
  14. Invert loaves onto wire cooling rack and brush tops with butter.
  15. Allow to cool for 10 minutes before slicing, then store in an airtight container.
Dutch Oven Bread
  • 3 cups bread flour
  • 2 tsp active dry yeast
  • 2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1½ cups very warm tap water
  1. Mix flour, yeast and salt in a large bowl.
  2. Add water and stir with the handle of a wooden spoon until all flour is incorporated.
  3. Cover with cling wrap or a plate and let rise for 2-3 hours until doubled in size.
  4. Preheat oven to 450°F with Dutch oven inside.
  5. Sprinkle flour over the dough ball and form it into a ball on parchment paper.
  6. Lift paper and dough into Dutch oven and return to oven.
  7. Bake 30 minutes covered, then 12 minutes uncovered.
  8. Remove and place on cooling rack for 10 minutes before slicing.
Garlic Herb Focaccia
  • 2 cups (480ml) warm water (100-110°F/38-43°C)
  • 2 tsp granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp instant or active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 Tbsp kosher salt
  • 4.5-5 cups (563-625g) all-purpose or bread flour
  • Toppings:
  • 5 Tbsp (75ml) extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3-4 Tbsp chopped fresh herbs (basil, thyme, rosemary) or 2 Tbsp dried herbs
  • Coarse salt and freshly ground black pepper
  1. Whisk water, sugar and yeast in a stand mixer bowl. Rest for 5 minutes.
  2. Add remaining water, olive oil, salt and 1 cup flour. Beat on low for 20 seconds.
  3. Add 3.5 cups more flour. Beat on low for 2 minutes.
  4. Knead dough for 5 minutes or until soft and slightly tacky.
  5. Let dough rise at room temperature for 2-3 hours or until doubled in size.
  6. Grease a 12x17-inch baking pan with olive oil.
  7. Punch down dough, flatten and fit to pan. Let rest for 5-10 minutes if shrinking.
  8. Refrigerate dough for 1-24 hours.
  9. Preheat oven to 450°F (232°C).
  10. Prepare toppings: whisk olive oil, garlic and herbs.
  11. Dimple dough, drizzle with olive oil mixture and sprinkle with salt and pepper.
  12. Bake for 20-23 minutes or until lightly browned.
  13. Optional: broil for 1 minute to brown top.
  14. Cut and serve hot or at room temperature.

Crispy Roast Potatoes

Ingredients:

  • 2 liters water
  • 2 tbsp salt
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • 4 lbs Yukon Gold or Russet potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 5 tbsp olive oil or animal fat
  • Small handful chopped fresh rosemary leaves
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Black pepper
  • Small handful minced fresh parsley leaves

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 450°F.
  2. Boil water in a large pot, then add salt, baking soda and potatoes.
  3. Return to boil, reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes or until potatoes are easily pierced with a knife.
  4. In a saucepan, combine oil/fat, rosemary, garlic and pepper. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until garlic is golden (about 3 minutes).
  5. Strain oil through a fine mesh into a large bowl, retaining both parts.
  6. Drain potatoes and let rest in pot for moisture to evaporate.
  7. Transfer potatoes to bowl with infused oil, season with salt and pepper, and shake to coat and create a thick paste.
  8. Spread potatoes evenly on a baking sheet and roast undisturbed for 20 minutes.
  9. Remove from oven, turn potatoes with a spatula and continue roasting for 35 minutes, turning and shaking occasionally.
  10. Transfer potatoes to a bowl, add herbs (including parsley) and toss to coat. Serve.
Potato Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1½ cups yellow potatoes, cubed
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 cup chopped snap peas or green beans
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Fresh dill, chopped (to taste)
  • Black pepper (to taste)

Instructions:

  1. Cover potatoes with water in a large pot, add salt.
  2. Cover and bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium and simmer uncovered until fork-tender (10-12 minutes).
  3. Drain potatoes and return to pot (off heat).
  4. Add mustard, mayonnaise, snap peas/green beans, green onions and dill. Season with pepper.
  5. Stir to coat and combine.
Potato Dauphinoise

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2+ cups Gruyère cheese, grated
  • 2½ lbs Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups warmed heavy cream
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F with rack in the middle.
  2. Coat a 4-quart baking dish with butter.
  3. Slice potatoes into ¼-inch thick rounds and place in a large pot.
  4. Add garlic, cream, milk, salt, pepper, nutmeg and bay leaves. Simmer over medium heat, stirring often, until potatoes are fork-tender (5-7 minutes).
  5. Remove bay leaves.
  6. Transfer half the potatoes to the baking dish, arranging in an even layer. Top with half the grated cheese.
  7. Arrange remaining potatoes on top, then pour enough cream mixture to reach just below the top layer.
  8. Top with remaining cheese and bake until golden-brown and easily pierced (about 45 minutes).
  9. Garnish with thyme and let rest 15 minutes on a wire rack before serving.

Soup

Broccoli Cheddar Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 + 4 tbsp butter, divided
  • 1 yellow onion, diced thin
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 2 cups cream, warmed
  • 3 cups broccoli, chopped thin
  • 2 carrots, sliced thin
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • ¾ tsp pepper
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp dry mustard
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper
  • 8 oz sharp cheddar, grated

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the onion and first portion of butter in a saucepan over medium heat until onions become translucent, adding garlic near the end, then remove from heat.
  2. Add the rest of the butter to a large pot, whisking in flour over 4 minutes to create a thick roux.
  3. Slowly whisk in chicken stock, followed by warmed cream.
  4. Simmer over low heat for 15-20 minutes to thicken, whisking in any skin that forms.
  5. Stir in the sautéed onion mixture, broccoli, carrots, salt, pepper, paprika, mustard, and cayenne.
  6. Simmer for 20-25 minutes, whisking in skin as the soup reduces.
  7. Stir in grated cheese until fully melted, about 1 minute, then serve.
Golden Chicken Noodle Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp canola oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 8 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1 bunch scallions, thinly sliced and separated by color
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Sea salt to taste
  • 2 skin-on, bone-in chicken breasts
  • ¼ lb thin round rice noodles, broken into inch-long pieces
  • 4 stalks celery, chopped
  • ⅓ cup fresh dill, finely chopped

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  2. Add onion, ginger, garlic, and scallion whites, and cook until softened, about 3 minutes.
  3. Add turmeric and black pepper, stirring until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  4. Add water and bay leaf, lightly seasoning with salt.
  5. Bring to a simmer, then add chicken and reduce heat to low.
  6. Cover and cook until chicken is cooked through, about 20 minutes.
  7. Transfer chicken to a cutting board and let rest until cool enough to handle.
  8. Remove and discard skin and bones, then shred meat into bite-sized pieces.
  9. Raise heat to medium and add noodles to the pot.
  10. Cook uncovered, stirring occasionally, until noodles are springy but still slightly firm, about 8 minutes.
  11. Return chicken to the pot along with celery, and cook until celery is bright green and noodles are fully cooked, about 4 minutes more.
  12. Remove from heat and stir in chopped dill and half the scallion greens.
  13. Serve with remaining scallion greens on top.
Chicken Tortellini

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless/skinless chicken thigh
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp pepper
  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • ⅓ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp dried basil
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 28 oz can diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups tortellini
  • 3 cups spinach
  • ½ cup parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup cream, warmed

Instructions:

  1. (NOTE: If planning for leftovers, prepare tortellini separately to prevent sogginess.)
  2. Season chicken with salt and pepper and let refrigerate while preparing other ingredients.
  3. Fry onions and garlic in olive oil until translucent.
  4. Mix in flour, basil, and tomato paste before adding to the slow cooker.
  5. Stir in broth, diced tomatoes, and seasoned chicken into the slow cooker.
  6. Cover and cook on LOW for 4-6 hours, or HIGH for 3-4 hours.
  7. Remove chicken and shred, then return to slow cooker along with tortellini, parmesan cheese, and warmed cream.
  8. Cook on HIGH for 10 more minutes, or until tortellini are cooked through, adding spinach halfway through.

Kimchi

Ingredients:

  • 10 lbs baechu (napa cabbage)
  • 1 cup kosher salt
  • ½ cup sweet rice flour
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • Water
  • 1 cup crushed garlic
  • 1-2 tbsp minced ginger
  • 1 cup minced onion
  • 1 cup fish sauce
  • Salty, fermented squid (see FAQ)
  • 2½ cups Korean hot pepper flakes (gochugaru)
  • 2 cups chopped leek
  • 10 green onions (diagonally sliced)
  • ¼ cup julienned carrot
  • 2 cups julienned Korean radish

Instructions:

  1. Trim discolored outer leaves from cabbage.
  2. Cut cabbage lengthwise into quarters, remove cores and chop into bite-sized pieces.
  3. Soak cabbage in cold water, then sprinkle with salt.
  4. Turn cabbage every 30 minutes for 1½ hours.
  5. Rinse cabbage in cold water 3 times and drain.
  6. Make porridge: combine 3 cups water and sweet rice flour, bring to a boil, then add sugar.
  7. Cool porridge, then mix with fish sauce, hot pepper flakes, garlic, ginger, onion, squid, green onions, leek, Korean radish and carrot.
  8. Combine kimchi paste with cabbage and mix by hand.
  9. Transfer to an airtight container or glass jar.
  10. Refrigerate or ferment.
Spice Rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 small red pepper, diced
  • ½ tsp chili flakes
  • 2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 2½ cups chicken stock
  • ½ cup frozen peas
  • 1½ cups rice

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a large saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Add onion and pepper, stirring constantly until tender.
  3. Add chili flakes, turmeric, cumin and paprika. Combine for 1 minute.

  7. Uncover and serve hot.

Pineapple Guacamole

Ingredients:

  • 4 discs fresh pineapple, cored
  • 3 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 limes, juiced
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1½ tsp chili powder
  • 4 avocados, peeled and pitted
  • 1½ cups finely chopped red onion
  • ½ cup diced tomato
  • 1 minced jalapeño
  • 1 clove minced garlic
  • ½ tsp coarse sea salt/kosher salt
  • ¼ cup finely chopped fresh cilantro leaves
  • Dash cumin

Instructions:

  1. Combine lime juice, brown sugar, honey and chili powder for pineapple glaze.

Chicken Parmesan

Marinara Sauce Ingredients:

  • 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 6 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 (28-oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 large sprigs basil
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Marinara Sauce Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pot over medium heat.
  2. Add onion, stir in garlic, reduce heat to low.
  3. Cover and cook, stirring occasionally, for 10 minutes.
  4. Uncover and cook another 5 minutes.
  5. Stir in tomatoes and basil, bring to a boil, then simmer (about 30 minutes).
  6. Discard basil, season with salt and pepper.

Chicken Ingredients:

  • 1 c panko bread crumbs
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 c finely grated Parmesan (divided)
  • 3/4 c all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 6 (1/4"-thick) boneless, skinless chicken cutlets (1 1/2 lb total)
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 c shredded mozzarella
  • 1 c vegetable or canola oil
  • Chopped fresh basil (for serving)

Chicken Instructions:

  1. Prepare 4 bowls: flour, eggs + water, panko + garlic powder + Parmesan, mozzarella + remaining Parmesan.
  2. Flatten chicken, pat dry, season with salt and pepper.
  3. Dip each breast into flour, egg, then panko.
  4. Heat oil to 350°, fry chicken until golden brown (1 minute per side).
  5. Transfer to prepared rack, repeat with remaining chicken.
  6. Spoon marinara over chicken, sprinkle cheese mixture.
  7. Broil until cheese melts and brown spots form (about 2 minutes).
  8. Top with basil, serve.

Tuscan Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 ½ cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 3 cups baby spinach
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • ¼ cup freshly grated Parmesan
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add chicken, season with salt, pepper and oregano. Cook until golden (8 minutes per side).
  3. Remove chicken, set aside.
  4. Melt butter in the same skillet. Stir in garlic (1 minute).
  5. Add cherry tomatoes, season with salt and pepper. Cook until tomatoes burst.
  6. Add spinach, cook until wilted.
  7. Stir in heavy cream and Parmesan. Simmer until sauce reduces (3 minutes).
  8. Return chicken to skillet, cook until heated through (5-7 minutes).
  9. Serve with lemon wedges.

Firecracker Shrimp

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise

  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1-2 cups flavorless oil (for frying)

  • 2 green onions, chopped

Meatballs

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • ½ lb ground pork
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • ½ cup bread crumbs
  • 1 egg
  • 5 tbsp whole milk
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Dash olive oil
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 3 tbsp flour
  • 2/3 cup vegetable stock
  • 2/3 cup beef stock
  • 2/3 cup thick double cream
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard

Instructions:

  1. Combine beef and pork in a bowl; break up lumps with fingers.
  2. Add onion, garlic, breadcrumbs, egg; mix well.
  3. Add milk; season with salt and pepper.
  4. Roll into small meatballs; refrigerate for 2 hours.
  5. Heat oil in a frying pan; brown meatballs on all sides.
  6. Transfer to ovenproof dish; cover and bake at 350°F (180°C) for 30 minutes.
  7. Melt butter in a pan; whisk in flour.
  8. Add vegetable and beef stock; stir continuously.
  9. Add double cream, soy sauce and Dijon mustard.
  10. Simmer until sauce thickens; drizzle over meatballs.

Tips:

  • Use high-quality ground meat for best flavor.
  • Chill meatballs before cooking for better texture.
  • Adjust gravy seasoning to taste.
  • Serve with lingonberry jam and mashed potatoes for authentic IKEA experience.

  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 tbsp water
  • ¾ cup peas

Instructions:

  1. Combine flour, garlic powder, salt and pepper; toss beef to coat.
  2. Heat oil in large pot; sear coated beef until browned.
  3. Remove beef; add onion and wine, scraping pan bottom.
  4. Add broth, return beef, and add potatoes, carrots, celery, tomato paste and rosemary.

  7. Stir in peas; simmer 5-10 minutes.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Tips:

  • Adjust seasoning to taste.
  • Serve with crusty bread or over mashed potatoes.

  • 350g/12 oz beef sausages, meat removed from casings

  • 1 cup pinot noir red wine (or 0% alcohol red wine/extra chicken stock)
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken stock/broth
  • 400g/14 oz can crushed tomatoes
  • 3 thyme sprigs (or 1/2 tsp dried)
  • 2 bay leaves (fresh or dried)
  • 1/2 tsp cooking salt/kosher salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp chilli flakes (red pepper flakes), optional
  • 500g/1 lb pappardelle pasta
  • Parmesan/Parmigiano Reggiano, finely grated
  • Parsley, finely chopped (optional garnish)

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C (160°C fan-forced).

  10. Serve: garnish with Parmesan and parsley (if using).

Chocolate Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2¼ cups (280g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1½ tsp cornstarch
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¾ cup (170g) unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • ¾ cup (150g) packed brown sugar
  • ½ cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg + 1 egg yolk
  • 2 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1¼ cups (225g) semi-sweet chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Whisk dry ingredients; set aside.
  2. Whisk wet ingredients; combine with dry ingredients.
  3. Fold in chocolate chips.
  4. Chill dough 2-3 hours or up to 3 days.
  5. Softening dough 10 minutes at room temperature.
  6. Preheat oven 325°F (163°C); line baking sheets.
  7. Scoop dough; shape into balls.
  8. Bake 12-14 minutes or until lightly browned.
  9. Cool 10 minutes; transfer to wire rack.

Tips:

  • Chill dough overnight for less spreading.
  • Use room temperature eggs.
  • Cookies stay fresh up to 1 week at room temperature.
Banana Bread

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ½ cup butter
  • ¾ cup brown sugar
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2⅓ cups bananas, mashed

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Lightly grease a loaf pan.
  3. Mix flour, baking soda and salt in a large bowl.
  4. Cream butter and brown sugar in a separate bowl.
  5. Stir eggs and bananas into butter mixture.
  6. Combine wet and dry ingredients; pour into loaf pan.
  7. Bake 60-65 minutes or until toothpick comes out clean.
  8. Cool in pan 10 minutes; transfer to wire rack.

Tips:

  • Ripe bananas yield best flavor.
  • Don't overmix batter.
  • Store cooled bread in airtight container up to 3 days.

Cinnamon Rolls

Dough Ingredients:

  • 2¼ tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warmed milk
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ⅓ cup butter
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 4 cups flour

Filling Ingredients:

  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 3 tbsp cinnamon
  • ⅓ cup softened unsalted butter

Icing Ingredients:

  • 6 tbsp softened unsalted butter
  • 1½ cups icing sugar
  • ¼ cup room temp cream cheese
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/8 tsp salt

Instructions:

  1. Combine yeast and milk; let sit 5 minutes.
  2. Mix sugar, butter, salt, eggs and flour in separate bowl.
  3. Combine bowls; knead until dough detaches from bowl.
  4. Rest dough 1 hour, covered with wet towel.
  5. Mix brown sugar and cinnamon; butter 9x13" dish.
  6. Roll dough 16x12x¼"; butter and sprinkle sugar.
  7. Roll up; cut into 1½" sections.
  8. Proof 30 minutes; bake 350°F 20+ minutes.
  9. Mix icing ingredients; spread over baked rolls.

Tips:

  • Use warm milk for yeast activation.
  • Don't overmix dough.
  • Let rolls cool before icing.
Chocolate Fudge Brownies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 cups white sugar
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract
  • 4 eggs
  • 1½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F; grease 9x13" baking dish.
  2. Combine melted butter, sugar and vanilla.
  3. Beat in eggs one at a time until blended.
  4. Sift flour, cocoa powder and salt.
  5. Gradually stir flour mixture into egg mixture.
  6. Stir in 1 cup chocolate chips.
  7. Spread batter into prepared dish.
  8. Top with remaining 1 cup chocolate chips.
  9. Bake 35-40 minutes or until toothpick comes out clean.
  10. Cool on wire rack before cutting.

Tips:

  • Use high-quality cocoa powder for best flavor.
  • Don't overbake; brownies should be fudgy.
  • Let cool completely for cleanest cuts.
DoubleTree Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1½ cups butter
  • 1½ cups white sugar
  • ¾ cups packed brown sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 2½ tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cups rolled oats
  • ¾ tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 3 cups semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1½ cups chopped walnuts

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Cream butter in large bowl; add sugars and beat 2 minutes.
  3. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each.
  4. Add vanilla and lemon juice; continue mixing.
  5. Mix flour, oats, baking soda, salt and cinnamon in separate bowl.
  6. Add dry ingredients to creamed mixture; blend.
  7. Stir in chocolate chips and walnuts.
  8. Drop ¼ cup scoops onto parchment-lined baking pans (2½ inches apart).
  9. Bake 13-15 minutes or until lightly browned around edges.
  10. Cool; remove from paper and transfer to wire rack.
